Allama Muhammad Iqbal Life History


  • Early Life and Education

    Born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, Punjab, British India (now in Pakistan)

    Received early education in Sialkot

    Studied at Government College, Lahore, and Trinity College, Cambridge

  • Advocacy for Muslim Rights

    Staunch advocate of the rights and freedoms of the Muslim community in India

    Believed in the need for Muslims to establish a separate homeland to protect their interests and safeguard their cultural heritage

  • Literary Achievements

    Renowned poet and philosopher

    Considered one of the greatest poets of the Urdu language

    Works explore themes of spirituality, faith, and the struggle for self-realization

    Famous works include "Asrar-e-Khudi", "Bang-i-Dra", and "Zarb-i-Kalim"

  • Political Activism

    Prominent member of the All India Muslim League

    Played a key role in the movement for the creation of Pakistan

    Delivered famous 1930 speech proposing the idea of a separate Muslim state in the Indian subcontinent (the "Allama Iqbal's Allahabad Address")

  • Legacy and Death

    Contributions to Urdu literature and the creation of Pakistan have made him a beloved figure in Pakistan and around the world

    Philosophy of self-realization and spiritual enlightenment continues to inspire people to this day

    Passed away on April 21, 1938, at the age of 60, but his legacy lives on in his poetry, philosophy, and political activism
